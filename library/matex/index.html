Understanding the Quadratic Formula

The quadratic formula is used to find the solutions (or roots) of a quadratic equation of the form:

\( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \)

The formula to find the roots is:

\[ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \]

Here's what each part represents:

  • a, b, and c are coefficients from the quadratic equation.
  • \( \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac} \) is called the discriminant.
  • The \( \pm \) symbol means there are generally two solutions: one with + and one with -.

For example, if we have the equation \( 2x^2 + 4x - 6 = 0 \), we plug in the values:

\[ x = \frac{-4 \pm \sqrt{(4)^2 - 4(2)(-6)}}{2(2)} = \frac{-4 \pm \sqrt{64}}{4} = \frac{-4 \pm 8}{4} \]

So the two solutions are \( x = 1 \) and \( x = -3 \). \(x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\)
